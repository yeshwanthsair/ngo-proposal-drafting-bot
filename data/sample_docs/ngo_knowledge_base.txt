SECTION 1: NGO GRANT PROPOSAL WRITING GUIDE
============================================================

INTRODUCTION
------------
A grant proposal is a formal document submitted to a funding organization (donor) requesting financial support for a specific project or program. For NGOs, writing effective proposals is critical to securing funding for their mission-driven work.

STANDARD PROPOSAL STRUCTURE
----------------------------

1. COVER PAGE
   - Organization name and logo
   - Project title
   - Funding amount requested
   - Project duration
   - Contact information
   - Date of submission

2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
   - Brief overview of the organization (2-3 sentences)
   - Problem statement summary
   - Proposed solution
   - Total budget requested
   - Expected impact
   Length: 1 page maximum

3. ORGANIZATIONAL BACKGROUND
   - History and mission of the NGO
   - Key programs and past achievements
   - Relevant experience and expertise
   - Governance structure
   - Financial health indicators

4. PROBLEM STATEMENT / NEEDS ASSESSMENT
   - Clear description of the problem being addressed
   - Evidence and data supporting the need
   - Target population and geographic area
   - Root causes of the problem
   - Why this problem requires urgent attention

5. PROJECT OBJECTIVES
   - Overall goal (broad, long-term)
   - Specific objectives (SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
   - Expected outcomes and outputs
   - Theory of change

6. PROJECT METHODOLOGY / IMPLEMENTATION PLAN
   - Detailed activities for each objective
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Roles and responsibilities
   - Partnerships and collaborations
   - Risk assessment and mitigation

7. MONITORING AND EVALUATION (M&E)
   - Key performance indicators (KPIs)
   - Data collection methods
   - Evaluation framework
   - Reporting schedule

8. BUDGET
   - Detailed line-item budget
   - Budget narrative/justification
   - Cost-effectiveness analysis
   - Other funding sources (co-funding)

9. SUSTAINABILITY PLAN
   - How the project will continue after grant period
   - Future funding strategies
   - Community ownership plans

10. ANNEXURES
    - Organization registration documents
    - Audited financial statements
    - CVs of key personnel
    - Letters of support from partners
    - Previous project reports

TIPS FOR SUCCESSFUL PROPOSALS
------------------------------
- Align your proposal with the donor's priorities and guidelines
- Use clear, simple language (avoid jargon)
- Provide concrete evidence and data
- Show your organization's track record
- Be realistic about what you can achieve
- Proofread carefully before submission
- Follow the donor's format exactly

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
-------------------------
- Vague objectives without measurable indicators
- Unrealistic timelines or budgets
- Ignoring donor guidelines
- Lack of evidence for the problem
- No sustainability plan
- Missing required documents

SECTION 2: NGO BUDGET PLANNING GUIDE
============================================================

BUDGET CATEGORIES FOR NGO PROJECTS
------------------------------------

1. PERSONNEL COSTS
   - Project Manager: Full-time or part-time salary
   - Field Officers/Community Workers
   - Finance Officer
   - M&E Officer
   - Administrative Staff
   Note: Include fringe benefits (health insurance, provident fund) typically 15-25% of salary

2. CONSULTANCY FEES
   - Technical experts
   - Trainers and facilitators
   - Evaluation consultants
   - Legal/audit services

3. TRAINING AND CAPACITY BUILDING
   - Workshop venue rental
   - Training materials and handouts
   - Participant travel and accommodation
   - Refreshments and meals
   - Facilitator fees

4. EQUIPMENT AND SUPPLIES
   - Computers and peripherals
   - Office furniture
   - Field equipment
   - Stationery and printing
   - Communication devices

5. TRAVEL AND TRANSPORTATION
   - Staff field visits
   - Vehicle hire or fuel costs
   - Air travel (if applicable)
   - Per diem allowances

6. COMMUNICATION
   - Phone and internet costs
   - Postage and courier
   - Website maintenance

7. PROGRAM ACTIVITIES
   - Community events
   - Awareness campaigns
   - Distribution of materials/kits
   - Beneficiary support

8. INDIRECT/OVERHEAD COSTS
   - Office rent and utilities
   - Organizational management
   - Typically 10-20% of direct costs
   - Some donors cap this at 15%

BUDGET JUSTIFICATION TIPS
--------------------------
- Explain why each cost is necessary
- Show how costs were calculated (unit cost x quantity)
- Demonstrate value for money
- Align costs directly to project activities
- Show any in-kind contributions

SAMPLE BUDGET FORMAT
--------------------
Item | Unit | Quantity | Unit Cost | Total Cost | Donor Funding | Own Contribution
Project Manager | Month | 12 | 50,000 | 600,000 | 480,000 | 120,000
Field Officers (2) | Month | 12 | 30,000 | 720,000 | 720,000 | 0
Training Workshop | Event | 4 | 25,000 | 100,000 | 100,000 | 0

FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------
- Maintain separate bank account for grant funds
- Keep all receipts and invoices
- Submit financial reports on schedule (monthly/quarterly)
- Allow donor audits
- Return unspent funds as per agreement
- Follow procurement policies

KEY FINANCIAL RATIOS DONORS LOOK FOR
--------------------------------------
- Program costs vs. administrative costs (aim for 70:30 or better)
- Cost per beneficiary
- Cost per outcome achieved

SECTION 3: SAMPLE GRANT PROPOSAL
============================================================

Project Title: Digital Literacy for Rural Women
Organization: Empowerment Foundation India
Funding Requested: INR 15,00,000
Project Duration: 12 months (April 2025 - March 2026)
Target Location: 5 villages, Krishnagiri District, Tamil Nadu

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
Empowerment Foundation India (EFI) requests INR 15,00,000 from the Digital India Foundation to implement a 12-month digital literacy program for 500 rural women in Krishnagiri District, Tamil Nadu. Despite government initiatives, 78% of rural women in our target area lack basic digital skills, limiting their access to government services, financial inclusion, and economic opportunities. Our program will train 500 women in smartphone usage, internet safety, digital payments, and accessing government schemes. By project end, we expect 400 women (80%) to independently use digital tools for livelihood improvement.

ORGANIZATIONAL BACKGROUND
--------------------------
Empowerment Foundation India was established in 2010 with the mission to advance gender equality and women's economic empowerment in rural Tamil Nadu. Over 14 years, EFI has:
- Reached 25,000+ women across 3 districts
- Implemented 12 successful projects with national and international donors
- Maintained 100% financial compliance record
- Won the State Women Empowerment Award (2022)

PROBLEM STATEMENT
-----------------
Rural women in Krishnagiri face a severe digital divide:
- 78% have never used a smartphone independently (EFI baseline survey, 2024)
- Only 12% have a bank account linked to mobile (RBI data, 2023)
- 65% are unaware of government welfare schemes they qualify for
- Women who use digital tools earn 34% more than those who don't (ILO, 2023)

Root causes: lack of access to devices, low literacy, social norms discouraging women's technology use, and absence of local-language digital content.

PROJECT OBJECTIVES
------------------
Overall Goal: Reduce the digital gender gap in Krishnagiri District

Specific Objectives:
1. Train 500 rural women in basic digital literacy within 12 months
2. Enable 400 women to independently access at least 3 government digital services
3. Support 200 women to use digital payment tools for business transactions
4. Build capacity of 20 local women as community digital champions

IMPLEMENTATION PLAN
--------------------
Phase 1 (Months 1-2): Baseline assessment, community mobilization, trainer recruitment
Phase 2 (Months 3-8): Training delivery - 25 batches of 20 women each
  - Module 1: Smartphone basics (2 days)
  - Module 2: Internet and safety (2 days)
  - Module 3: Government services - DigiLocker, UMANG app (2 days)
  - Module 4: Digital payments - UPI, banking apps (2 days)
Phase 3 (Months 9-11): Advanced support, business digital tools, mentoring
Phase 4 (Month 12): Evaluation, documentation, sustainability handover

MONITORING AND EVALUATION
--------------------------
Key Performance Indicators:
- Number of women trained (target: 500)
- % who pass digital skills assessment (target: 80%)
- Number using government apps independently (target: 400)
- Number using digital payments (target: 200)
- Number of community champions trained (target: 20)

Data collection: Pre/post assessments, monthly tracking sheets, focus group discussions
External evaluation: Mid-term (Month 6) and final (Month 12)

BUDGET SUMMARY
--------------
Personnel (Project Manager, 2 Trainers, Field Officers): INR 6,00,000
Training Materials and Devices (tablets for training centers): INR 3,50,000
Venue and Logistics: INR 1,50,000
Community Mobilization: INR 1,00,000
M&E and Documentation: INR 75,000
Overhead (10%): INR 1,25,000
TOTAL: INR 15,00,000 (Donor: 13,50,000 | EFI Contribution: 1,50,000)

SUSTAINABILITY PLAN
-------------------
- 20 trained community champions will continue peer training
- Partner with local Panchayat to integrate into CSC activities
- Develop Tamil-language training materials for ongoing use
- Seek government convergence funding for Year 2 scale-up
